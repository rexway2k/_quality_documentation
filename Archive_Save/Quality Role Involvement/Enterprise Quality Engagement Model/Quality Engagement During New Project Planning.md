# Quality Engagement During New Project Planning

## Purpose

Quality involvement should begin as early as possible during project planning and solution design.

The objective is not simply to prepare for testing activities. The objective is to establish a clear understanding of the desired outcomes, identify risks and dependencies, align expectations, and develop a quality strategy that supports successful delivery.

Early quality engagement helps prevent defects, reduce rework, improve planning accuracy, and establish confidence before implementation begins.

Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals should be active participants throughout project planning activities and seek involvement whenever sufficient information is not available to understand the scope, risk, or complexity of a proposed solution.

---

## Quality Objectives During Project Planning

During project planning activities, quality professionals should seek to understand:

- The business problem being solved
- The desired outcome and value being delivered
- The consumers and users impacted by the change
- Current-state and future-state business processes
- Technical architecture and implementation approaches
- Risks, assumptions, constraints, and dependencies
- Integration points and data flows
- Operational and support considerations
- Release expectations and delivery timelines

A quality professional should be able to explain not only what is being built, but why it is being built, who it impacts, how it will work, and what could potentially fail.

---

## Recommended Quality Participation Opportunities

Quality professionals should seek inclusion, consultation, or active participation in the following activities when they occur.

### Business Vision and Solution Planning

Participate when Product Owners, Business Analysts, stakeholders, or delivery teams discuss:

- Business objectives
- Desired outcomes
- Problem statements
- Current-state versus future-state workflows
- Product vision and roadmap planning
- Epic envisioning sessions
- Feature discovery activities

Quality professionals should ensure consumer expectations and quality considerations are discussed early.

---

### User Experience and Process Design

Participate when:

- UI/UX designs are reviewed
- Wireframes and prototypes are presented
- Customer journeys are discussed
- Current-state and future-state user experiences are evaluated

These sessions help identify usability risks, workflow concerns, accessibility considerations, and opportunities for earlier validation.

---

### Architecture and Technical Design Reviews

Participate when:

- Architectural designs are reviewed
- Technical implementation approaches are discussed
- Integration patterns are identified
- Infrastructure requirements are evaluated
- Data flow discussions occur
- System boundaries and dependencies are identified

Understanding solution architecture allows quality professionals to better assess risk and design effective validation strategies.

---

### Delivery and Estimation Activities

Participate when:

- High-level project estimation occurs
- T-shirt sizing exercises occur
- Delivery timelines are discussed
- Capacity planning activities occur
- Resource dependencies are being evaluated

Quality professionals should help ensure quality activities are appropriately considered during planning exercises.

---

### Risk Assessment Activities

Participate whenever teams discuss:

- Business risk
- Technical risk
- Compliance concerns
- Data integrity risks
- Security considerations
- Operational readiness
- Release risk

Risk discussions should influence testing approaches, quality strategies, and validation priorities.

---

### Dependency and Cross-Team Planning

Participate when:

- Cross-team dependencies are identified
- Shared services or platforms are impacted
- Integration testing may be required
- Data exchange between systems exists
- Business units require coordinated delivery

Quality professionals should help ensure end-to-end validation efforts are identified early.

---

### Release Strategy Discussions

Participate when:

- Release plans are developed
- Deployment approaches are discussed
- Rollback plans are reviewed
- Production readiness expectations are established

Early involvement allows quality planning and release planning to remain aligned throughout delivery.

---

### Retrospectives and Lessons Learned

Participate when teams review:

- Previous project outcomes
- Production incidents
- Release challenges
- Process improvement opportunities
- Quality observations and trends

Lessons learned should influence future planning and delivery approaches.

---

## Quality Responsibilities During Project Planning

Quality professionals are expected to contribute the following during project planning phases.

### Establish Quality Strategy

Develop and communicate a quality strategy appropriate to the size, complexity, and risk profile of the initiative.

This may include:

- Functional testing approaches
- Integration testing approaches
- End-to-end testing approaches
- Regression testing approaches
- Automation strategies
- Non-functional testing needs
- Performance and load testing considerations
- Operational validation requirements

---

### Identify Risks and Failure Modes

Support the team in identifying:

- Areas of elevated business or technical risk
- Potential failure scenarios
- Data integrity concerns
- Dependency risks
- Testing constraints
- Production support considerations

The goal is not to eliminate risk but to understand, prioritize, and manage it.

---

### Align Quality and Development Strategies

Quality planning should align closely with the team's implementation approach.

Quality professionals should understand:

- Development methodologies
- Technical design decisions
- Deployment strategies
- Architectural patterns
- Testability considerations

This alignment allows testing activities, automation efforts, and validation approaches to support delivery goals effectively.

---

### Plan Cross-Team Validation Activities

When multiple systems, teams, vendors, or business units are involved, quality professionals should help coordinate:

- End-to-end testing strategies
- Shared testing environments
- Data needs
- Integration validation activities
- Release coordination efforts

Ownership and coordination of end-to-end validation should be established as early as possible.

---

### Ensure Readiness for Delivery

Quality professionals should help establish confidence that:

- Expectations are understood
- Success criteria are defined
- Testing approaches are identified
- Major risks are known
- Dependencies are documented
- Validation activities can be executed successfully

---

## Expected Outputs

Project planning activities should produce enough information for quality professionals to establish:

- A documented quality strategy
- Preliminary testing approaches
- Risk assessments
- Dependency considerations
- Automation opportunities
- End-to-end testing requirements
- Release validation strategies
- Environment and test data needs

These outputs will continue to evolve throughout delivery but should begin during project planning.

---

## Guiding Questions for Quality Professionals

During project planning, quality professionals should continuously seek answers to the following questions:

### Outcome

- What problem are we solving?
- What does success look like?

### Consumer

- Who will use this solution?
- What are their expectations?

### Constraints

- What limitations, dependencies, or assumptions exist?

### Structure

- How will the solution work?
- What systems, services, and teams are involved?

### Failure Modes

- What could go wrong?
- What represents the greatest risk?

### Verification

- How will we know the solution satisfies expectations?
- What evidence will provide confidence for release?

These questions establish the foundation for effective quality planning and align with Clayton Tech's Quality Container framework.

---

## Success Criteria

Effective quality engagement during project planning results in:

- Clear business and consumer expectations
- Early identification of risks and dependencies
- Better alignment between delivery and quality strategies
- Improved testing readiness
- Stronger end-to-end validation planning
- Increased delivery confidence
- Reduced downstream rework
- Fewer surprises during implementation and release

The goal of quality engagement during project planning is to establish clarity, reduce uncertainty, and create a foundation for successful delivery.
