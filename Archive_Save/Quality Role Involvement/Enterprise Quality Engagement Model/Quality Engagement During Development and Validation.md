# Quality Engagement During Development and Validation

## Purpose

Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals remain actively engaged throughout implementation and validation activities during the sprint.

Quality activities should occur in parallel with development efforts rather than being treated as a separate phase that begins after coding is complete.

The purpose of quality engagement during development is to continuously validate assumptions, identify risks, provide rapid feedback, improve delivery confidence, and ensure solutions continue progressing toward meeting business and consumer expectations.

Quality professionals should work closely with Product Owners, Software Engineers, Architects, and stakeholders throughout implementation to support successful delivery outcomes.

---

## Quality Objectives During Development and Validation

Throughout implementation activities, quality professionals should seek to:

- Maintain visibility into development progress
- Understand completed and upcoming implementation work
- Continuously evaluate quality risks
- Validate solutions against business expectations
- Support development teams through rapid feedback loops
- Ensure testing activities remain aligned with implementation progress
- Increase delivery confidence through validation and automation
- Promote shared ownership of quality across the team

Quality should be a continuous activity throughout the sprint, not a final checkpoint before release.

---

## Recommended Quality Participation Opportunities

Quality professionals should maintain awareness and involvement during key implementation and validation activities.

### Active Development

Remain informed when:

- Work is actively being developed
- Technical solutions are changing
- Significant implementation decisions are made
- Requirements evolve
- Risks or concerns emerge

Early awareness enables earlier feedback and reduces downstream rework.

---

### Development Completion

Participate when work is considered development complete.

Quality professionals should ensure:

- Acceptance criteria are understood
- Validation activities are planned
- Automation opportunities are identified
- Risks remain visible

Development completion should create readiness for validation rather than transfer ownership.

---

### Code Review Activities

Maintain awareness of:

- Significant code reviews
- Architectural changes
- Complex implementations
- Design decisions that may impact testing or operational quality

While quality professionals are not necessarily code reviewers, awareness of implementation decisions improves testing effectiveness and risk assessment.

---

### Environment Promotion Activities

Maintain awareness of code movement across environments.

This may include:

- Integration environments
- Quality environments
- User acceptance testing environments
- Pre-production environments
- Production deployment readiness

Understanding what has been deployed and where it has been deployed supports effective validation and release confidence.

---

## Quality Responsibilities During Development and Validation

### Execute Validation Activities

Quality professionals are responsible for executing appropriate validation activities based on risk, requirements, and delivery expectations.

This may include:

- Functional testing
- Regression testing
- Integration testing
- End-to-end testing
- Exploratory testing
- User acceptance testing support
- Non-functional testing
- Security validation
- Performance validation
- Accessibility validation

Testing activities should provide meaningful confidence in delivered functionality.

---

### Develop and Maintain Test Assets

Quality professionals should create and maintain the assets necessary to support delivery.

This may include:

- Manual test cases
- Automated tests
- Test data
- Validation checklists
- Test execution documentation

Test assets should remain maintainable, reusable, and aligned with business objectives.

---

### Support Automation Efforts

Quality professionals should continuously evaluate automation opportunities.

Responsibilities may include:

- Creating new automated tests
- Maintaining existing automation assets
- Identifying regression candidates
- Identifying opportunities for earlier automated feedback
- Creating future backlog items when automation work cannot be completed during the current sprint

Automation efforts should focus on maximizing repeatability, confidence, and long-term efficiency.

---

### Collaborate Through Peer Reviews

Quality professionals should participate in peer review activities where appropriate.

This may include:

- Test case reviews
- Automation reviews
- Validation strategy reviews
- Quality plan reviews

Peer review improves consistency, coverage, and knowledge sharing across the quality organization.

---

### Identify and Manage Quality Issues

When deviations from expected behavior are discovered, quality professionals should:

- Document findings appropriately
- Communicate impacts and risks
- Collaborate on issue investigation
- Support root cause analysis efforts
- Verify resolution of identified issues

Effective issue management helps improve both immediate delivery outcomes and long-term quality maturity.

---

### Defect and Issue Management

Quality professionals should ensure defects and quality concerns are appropriately documented and tracked.

Defect management activities may include:

- Recording defects
- Tracking issue status
- Verifying resolutions
- Identifying root causes
- Assessing business impact
- Supporting prioritization discussions

Issue tracking should provide visibility into quality trends and improvement opportunities.

---

### Maintain Quality Metrics and Insights

Quality professionals should contribute to the collection and analysis of quality signals.

Examples include:

- Defect trends
- Root cause analysis
- Test coverage metrics
- Automation coverage
- Validation effectiveness
- Production quality indicators

Metrics should be used to support learning and continuous improvement rather than individual evaluation.

---

### Maintain Risk Visibility

Every change introduces potential risk.

Quality professionals should help ensure risks remain visible throughout implementation.

Risk activities may include:

- Reviewing risk scores
- Updating risk assessments
- Evaluating changes in business or technical impact
- Identifying emerging risks
- Supporting governance and review processes

Risk should be continuously assessed throughout delivery rather than evaluated only during release activities.

---

### Support Risk Register Maintenance

When new functionality, dependencies, architectural changes, or operational concerns emerge, quality professionals should collaborate with delivery and quality leadership teams to determine whether updates to organizational risk registers are appropriate.

Risk management should evolve alongside the solution being delivered.

---

## Expected Outputs

Development and validation activities should produce:

- Executed validation activities
- Documented testing evidence
- Manual and automated test assets
- Defect and issue documentation
- Updated risk assessments
- Quality metrics and reporting
- Root cause analysis information
- Release readiness inputs

These artifacts help establish confidence in delivery outcomes and support informed release decisions.

---

## Guiding Questions for Quality Professionals

During development and validation activities, quality professionals should continuously seek answers to the following questions.

### Outcome

- Is the implemented solution achieving the intended outcome?

### Consumer

- Does the solution meet consumer and stakeholder expectations?

### Constraints

- Have any new dependencies, assumptions, or risks emerged?

### Structure

- Is the implementation aligned with the intended design and architecture?

### Failure Modes

- What scenarios could still create customer, business, or operational impact?

### Verification

- What evidence demonstrates that expectations have been met?

These questions help quality professionals focus on outcomes, confidence, and risk management throughout delivery.

---

## Success Criteria

Effective quality engagement during development and validation results in:

- Continuous feedback throughout implementation
- Earlier identification of defects and risks
- Increased automation coverage
- Improved test effectiveness
- Reduced rework and delivery delays
- Greater release confidence
- Stronger collaboration across delivery teams
- Improved visibility into product quality and operational risk

The goal of quality engagement during development and validation is to continuously build confidence, reduce uncertainty, and help teams deliver solutions that meet expectations.
