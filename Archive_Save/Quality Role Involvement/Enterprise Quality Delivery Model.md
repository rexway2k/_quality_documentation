# Enterprise Quality Delivery Model

## Purpose

Tech teams use iterative and adaptive delivery practices to develop, validate, and release technology solutions.

Quality activities are integrated throughout the solution delivery lifecycle rather than being performed as a separate phase near the end of development. Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals work closely with Product Owners, Software Engineers, Architects, Business Stakeholders, and Support teams to establish confidence that solutions meet expectations and business objectives.

The goal of the Enterprise Quality Delivery Model is to ensure that quality is continuously considered, validated, and improved from initial planning through production support.

---

## Quality Philosophy

Quality is defined as the fulfillment of expectations.

Quality is not solely determined by the absence of defects. A solution is considered high quality when it successfully meets the needs of its consumers, addresses business objectives, manages risk appropriately, and performs reliably in real-world use.

Quality is everyone’s responsibility.

QA and QE professionals provide leadership, expertise, risk management, validation, and quality advocacy throughout solution delivery, but quality outcomes are shared responsibilities across the entire delivery team.

---

## Iterative Delivery and Continuous Validation

Tech teams primarily utilize Agile delivery practices and work in iterative development cycles that align with business and delivery needs.

Work is continuously refined, planned, implemented, validated, demonstrated, and improved through collaboration between business and technology teams.

Quality activities occur continuously throughout these iterations and are not limited to a specific testing phase.

Examples include:

- Requirements and acceptance criteria reviews
- Risk assessment activities
- Test strategy development
- Test design and test automation
- Functional and non-functional validation
- End-to-end testing
- Release readiness reviews
- Production validation and monitoring
- Continuous improvement activities

The earlier quality activities occur, the more effectively teams can manage risk and reduce rework.

---

## Collaborative Quality Delivery

Successful quality outcomes require active participation from the entire delivery team.

Throughout delivery, QA and QE professionals collaborate with:

- Product Owners
- Business Analysts
- Software Engineers
- Architects
- Business Stakeholders
- Support Teams
- Other Delivery Teams and Business Units

This collaboration helps establish shared expectations, improve understanding of business needs, identify risks early, and build confidence in release decisions.

Quality activities should occur alongside development activities rather than after development is completed.

---

## Risk-Based Validation

Testing and validation efforts should be aligned with the risk profile of the solution being delivered.

Quality professionals work with delivery teams to evaluate factors such as:

- Business impact
- Customer impact
- Technical complexity
- Integration dependencies
- Regulatory requirements
- Security considerations
- Operational risks
- Deployment risks

Higher-risk solutions may require additional validation activities, while lower-risk changes may require lighter-weight approaches.

The objective is not to eliminate all risk but to understand, communicate, and manage risk appropriately.

---

## Continuous Feedback and Demonstration

Throughout delivery, teams should continuously validate progress and gather feedback.

Feedback mechanisms may include:

- Team collaboration sessions
- Design reviews
- Technical reviews
- Sprint demonstrations
- User acceptance activities
- Release readiness reviews
- Production monitoring and feedback

Demonstrations and reviews provide opportunities for stakeholders to validate expectations, provide input, and confirm that solutions are progressing toward intended outcomes.

Feedback should be incorporated as early as possible to support continuous improvement and reduce downstream rework.

---

## Quality Engineering and Automation

Automation is an important component of any quality strategy.

Automation helps provide faster feedback, improve repeatability, increase coverage, and support continuous delivery practices.

Quality professionals should identify appropriate opportunities for automation throughout solution delivery, including:

- Unit validation support
- API testing
- User interface automation
- Regression testing
- End-to-end validation
- Smoke testing
- Monitoring and observability activities

Automation should support quality objectives and provide meaningful feedback. Automation does not replace critical thinking, exploratory testing, or collaboration.

---

## Production Quality and Continuous Improvement

Quality activities continue after deployment.

Production environments provide valuable feedback regarding:

- System performance
- Reliability
- User experience
- Defect trends
- Operational effectiveness
- Business outcomes

Quality professionals should use production insights to improve testing strategies, automation coverage, risk assessments, and delivery practices.

Every release provides an opportunity to improve future delivery outcomes.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Successful application of the Enterprise Quality Delivery Model should result in:

- Clearer expectations and requirements
- Earlier identification of risks
- Increased confidence in delivery decisions
- Improved collaboration across teams
- Reduced production defects and incidents
- Faster feedback loops
- Greater automation effectiveness
- Improved customer and stakeholder satisfaction
- Continuous improvement of products, processes, and practices

Quality is not a phase.

Quality is not a gate.

Quality is not owned by a single person or team.

**Quality is a continuous commitment to delivering outcomes that meet expectations.**
