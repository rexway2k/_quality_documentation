# AI Quality Assurance Standard

## Purpose

This standard defines enterprise expectations for validating AI-enabled solutions.

AI systems introduce risks beyond traditional software behavior.

---

# Objectives

Quality activities should evaluate:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Safety
- Fairness
- Robustness
- Monitoring readiness

---

# AI-Specific Risks

Potential risks include:

- Hallucination
- Bias
- Drift
- Prompt misuse
- Data quality issues
- Unintended outputs

---

# Validation Areas

## Functional Validation

Does the solution perform expected tasks?

---

## Prompt Validation

Do prompts consistently produce acceptable outcomes?

---

## Grounding Validation

Can outputs be verified against trusted sources?

---

## Boundary Testing

How does the solution respond to unusual inputs?

---

## Safety Validation

Can unsafe, harmful, or restricted outputs be generated?

---

# Human Oversight

Human review remains required for AI-assisted outputs used in decision-making.

AI is assistive, not authoritative.

---

# Monitoring

AI-enabled solutions should include monitoring for:

- Drift
- Quality degradation
- Unexpected behaviors

---

# Continuous Evaluation

AI validation should continue throughout the solution lifecycle and not be limited to initial release activities.
