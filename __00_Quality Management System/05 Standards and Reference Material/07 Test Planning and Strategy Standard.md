# Test Planning and Strategy Standard

## Purpose

This standard establishes enterprise expectations for planning quality activities and defining testing approaches.

Test planning exists to create intentional alignment between:

- Risk
- Coverage
- Confidence
- Business outcomes

Test planning is not intended to create documentation for its own sake.

---

# Guiding Principles

Testing is a risk reduction activity.

The goal is not exhaustive testing.

The goal is establishing sufficient confidence to support informed decisions.

Testing depth should be proportional to:

- Business impact
- Technical complexity
- Change scope
- Operational risk

---

# Required Planning Activities

## Outcome

What behavior must be validated?

What does success look like?

---

## Consumer

Who uses the solution?

Who is impacted by failure?

---

## Constraints

What business, technical, regulatory, or operational constraints exist?

---

## Structure

How does the solution function?

What components, integrations, or dependencies are involved?

---

## Failure Modes

What can fail?

What are the most likely and most damaging risks?

---

## Verification

How will confidence be established?

What evidence is required?

---

# Test Strategy Development

The testing strategy should define:

- Scope
- Exclusions
- Risk considerations
- Test levels
- Environment requirements
- Test data requirements
- Automation opportunities

---

# Required Test Layers

Validation should be considered across:

## Unit Testing

Fast feedback and code-level validation.

---

## Service/API Testing

Validation of contracts and integration points.

---

## Integration Testing

Validation of interactions between systems.

---

## Functional Testing

Validation of business workflows.

---

## End-to-End Testing

Validation of complete user journeys.

---

# Non-Functional Planning

Where applicable strategies should address:

- Performance
- Reliability
- Stability
- Scalability
- Security
- Resilience

---

# Test Exit Expectations

Prior to release teams should evaluate:

- Coverage achieved
- Risks remaining
- Defects remaining
- Test execution outcomes
- Automation outcomes

Remaining risks must be visible and intentional.
