# Metrics and Reporting Standard

## Purpose

This standard establishes enterprise quality measurements and reporting expectations.

Metrics are decision-support signals.

Metrics are not performance targets.

---

# Metric Principles

Metrics should:

- Encourage learning
- Drive visibility
- Inform decisions
- Identify risk

Metrics should not:

- Rank individuals
- Compare teams without context
- Incentivize unhealthy behavior

# Enterprise Metrics

## Release Quality Ratio

Measures quality outcomes after release.

---

## Defect Discovery Distribution

Measures where defects are identified.

Examples:

- Requirements
- Development
- Testing
- Production

---

## Root Cause Trends

Measures causes of recurring issues.

---

## Test Coverage

Measures validation coverage.

Coverage should include:

- Requirements
- Functional workflows
- Critical business processes

---

## Automation Proportion

Measures automated versus manual validation.

---

## Automation ROI

Measures value gained from automation investment.

---

# Reporting Cadence

Reports may be reviewed:

- Sprint
- Release
- Monthly
- Quarterly

based on organizational needs.

---

# Governance

Metrics must be interpreted alongside:

- Risk
- Context
- Architecture
- Delivery complexity

Metrics should never be used in isolation.
