# Quality Engagement During Backlog Refinement and Estimation

## Purpose

Backlog Refinement and Estimation activities provide an opportunity for Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals to influence quality before development work begins.

These activities help establish a shared understanding of requirements, identify risks and dependencies, assess testability, and improve delivery predictability.

Quality involvement during refinement helps ensure that work entering future sprints is understood, appropriately sized, testable, and aligned with business expectations.

The objective is not simply to estimate testing effort.

The objective is to reduce uncertainty, improve readiness, and establish confidence that the team understands both the expected outcome and the associated risks.

---

## Quality Objectives During Backlog Refinement and Estimation

During refinement activities, quality professionals should seek to understand:

- The business value of the work
- The desired outcome and success criteria
- Consumer and user expectations
- Acceptance criteria and business rules
- Technical implementation considerations
- Testing requirements and complexity
- Risks, assumptions, and constraints
- Cross-team and system dependencies
- Opportunities for automation and early validation

Quality professionals should continuously evaluate whether sufficient information exists to confidently validate the intended solution.

---

## Recommended Quality Participation Opportunities

Quality professionals should seek inclusion, consultation, or active participation during the following refinement activities.

### Review of Prioritized Work

Participate when upcoming work is reviewed and prioritized.

Quality professionals should understand:

- Why the work is important
- Expected business outcomes
- Consumer impact
- Relative risk

This context helps prioritize quality efforts and validation strategies.

---

### Requirements and Acceptance Criteria Discussions

Participate when teams review:

- User stories
- Features
- Epics
- Acceptance criteria
- Business rules
- Success measures

Quality professionals should help identify ambiguity, gaps, assumptions, and missing requirements before implementation begins.

Acceptance criteria should be sufficiently clear to support validation and release decisions.

---

### Technical Design and Implementation Discussions

Participate when teams discuss:

- Technical approaches
- Solution design
- Implementation strategies
- Architectural considerations
- Integration requirements
- Data flow changes

These discussions improve understanding of testing needs, complexity, and potential risk areas.

---

### Research and Discovery Activities

Participate when stories require additional investigation, spikes, proof-of-concepts, or technical research.

Quality professionals should help evaluate:

- Potential risk areas
- Unknowns
- Validation challenges
- Operational considerations
- Future testing needs

Understanding uncertainty early improves planning accuracy.

---

### Estimation Activities

Participate when teams estimate effort and complexity.

Quality professionals should contribute perspectives related to:

- Testing effort
- Validation complexity
- Automation effort
- Integration considerations
- Test data requirements
- Dependency impacts
- Environmental needs

Quality activities are an important component of overall delivery effort and should be considered during estimation discussions.

---

## Quality Responsibilities During Backlog Refinement and Estimation

### Contribute to Shared Understanding

Quality professionals should actively participate in discussions to help establish a common understanding of:

- Business intent
- Expected outcomes
- Consumer expectations
- Technical implementation considerations
- Potential risks

The quality perspective often reveals assumptions, edge cases, and failure scenarios that may otherwise be overlooked.

---

### Evaluate Testability

Quality professionals should help determine whether a backlog item is sufficiently defined and testable.

This includes evaluating:

- Acceptance criteria clarity
- Expected behavior
- Error handling expectations
- Data requirements
- Environment needs
- Validation approaches

Work that cannot be effectively validated should be further refined before implementation begins.

---

### Identify Risks and Failure Modes

Quality professionals should proactively identify:

- Technical risks
- Business risks
- Data integrity concerns
- Integration risks
- Dependency concerns
- User experience risks

Risk discussions should be visible to the entire team and influence implementation and validation approaches.

---

### Contribute to Estimation Discussions

Quality professionals should actively contribute to estimation conversations by identifying complexity associated with:

- Validation activities
- Automation requirements
- Integration testing
- Regression impacts
- End-to-end testing
- Non-functional testing needs

The purpose is not to estimate test execution alone but to understand the total quality effort required to deliver confidence.

---

### Align Automation Strategies

Quality professionals should identify opportunities for automation during refinement.

This may include:

- Unit testing opportunities
- API testing opportunities
- UI automation opportunities
- Regression automation candidates
- Monitoring and observability opportunities

Automation discussions should occur before development begins whenever possible.

---

### Coordinate Testing Dependencies

Quality professionals should identify and communicate:

- Cross-team testing needs
- Shared environment requirements
- Test data needs
- External system dependencies
- End-to-end validation requirements

Early coordination reduces delivery risk and improves planning accuracy.

---

### Understand Planned Unit Testing

Quality professionals should understand how Software Engineers plan to validate their work through:

- Unit testing
- Component testing
- Integration testing
- Automated validation

This understanding helps ensure appropriate test coverage without unnecessary duplication of effort.

Quality activities should complement development validation activities rather than replicate them.

---

## Expected Outputs

Backlog Refinement and Estimation should provide sufficient information for quality professionals to establish:

- Testability assessments
- Identified risks and assumptions
- Validation approaches
- Automation opportunities
- Dependency requirements
- Test data needs
- Initial testing strategies
- Relative quality complexity

Work entering sprint planning should have enough information available to support informed delivery decisions.

---

## Guiding Questions for Quality Professionals

During refinement and estimation activities, quality professionals should continuously seek answers to the following questions.

### Outcome

- What problem is this work intended to solve?
- What does success look like?

### Consumer

- Who is affected by this change?
- What expectations must be met?

### Constraints

- What dependencies, assumptions, or limitations exist?

### Structure

- How is the solution expected to work?
- What systems, integrations, and data flows are involved?

### Failure Modes

- What could go wrong?
- What scenarios represent the highest risk?

### Verification

- How will the team validate success?
- What evidence will establish confidence?

These questions support effective refinement activities and align with Clayton Tech's Quality Container framework.

---

## Success Criteria

Effective quality engagement during backlog refinement and estimation results in:

- Clearer acceptance criteria
- Improved story readiness
- Better visibility into risk and complexity
- Earlier identification of dependencies
- Improved estimation accuracy
- Stronger automation planning
- Improved collaboration across delivery teams
- Reduced implementation surprises
- Greater confidence entering sprint planning

The goal of quality engagement during backlog refinement and estimation is to improve understanding, reduce uncertainty, and ensure that upcoming work is ready for successful delivery.
