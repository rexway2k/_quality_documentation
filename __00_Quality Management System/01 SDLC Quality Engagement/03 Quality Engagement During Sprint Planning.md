# Quality Engagement During Sprint Planning

## Purpose

Sprint Planning provides an opportunity for Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals to align quality activities with the work planned for delivery.

Quality involvement during sprint planning ensures that business expectations, implementation approaches, risks, dependencies, and validation activities are understood before development begins. This allows quality efforts to be intentional, risk-based, and aligned with sprint objectives.

The goal is not simply to plan testing activities.

The goal is to establish confidence that the team has a shared understanding of what is being delivered, how success will be measured, and how quality risks will be managed throughout the sprint.

---

## Quality Objectives During Sprint Planning

During sprint planning, quality professionals should seek to understand:

- Sprint goals and intended outcomes
- Business value associated with planned work
- Consumer and user expectations
- Technical implementation approaches
- Risks and dependencies associated with sprint commitments
- Testing and validation requirements
- Release expectations and timelines
- Cross-team coordination needs

Quality professionals should ensure quality activities remain aligned with sprint priorities and delivery commitments.

---

## Recommended Quality Participation Opportunities

Quality professionals should seek inclusion, consultation, or active participation during the following sprint planning activities.

### Sprint Goal Definition

Participate when sprint objectives and commitments are discussed.

This allows quality professionals to understand:

- The intended outcome of the sprint
- Business priorities
- Areas of highest value
- Areas of highest risk

Understanding sprint goals helps prioritize quality efforts appropriately.

---

### Capacity and Availability Planning

Participate when teams assess:

- Team capacity
- Resource availability
- Planned leave
- Environmental constraints
- Testing resource requirements

Quality activities require sufficient time, environments, tooling, and collaboration to execute successfully.

---

### Review of Planned Work

Participate when backlog items are reviewed for sprint inclusion.

Quality professionals should help ensure:

- Acceptance criteria are understood
- Dependencies have been identified
- Validation requirements are considered
- Testing complexity is understood
- Quality risks are visible

Any ambiguity should be addressed before sprint commitments are finalized.

---

### Sprint Risk Discussions

Participate whenever sprint-level risks are reviewed.

Examples include:

- Technical uncertainty
- Complex integrations
- Shared dependencies
- Environment availability concerns
- Test data challenges
- Resource constraints
- Release timing risks

Risk discussions should influence the depth and scope of quality activities planned for the sprint.

---

### Work Allocation and Coordination

Participate when delivery activities are organized and responsibilities are discussed.

Quality professionals should understand:

- Development sequencing
- Dependency timing
- Validation needs
- Coordination points
- Expected testing windows

This helps avoid bottlenecks and supports smoother execution throughout the sprint.

---

### Release and Deployment Planning

Participate when deployment expectations are discussed.

Topics may include:

- Planned release timing
- Deployment coordination
- Validation expectations
- Rollback considerations
- Post-deployment verification activities

Quality planning should remain aligned with release planning throughout the sprint.

---

### Sprint Commitment Discussions

Participate when the team establishes or modifies sprint commitments.

Quality professionals should ensure:

- Validation activities are adequately considered
- Testing capacity is realistic
- Risks are understood
- Dependencies are acknowledged
- Confidence levels are transparent

The objective is not to limit commitments but to ensure commitments are informed and achievable.

---

## Quality Responsibilities During Sprint Planning

### Develop Sprint Quality Plans

Quality professionals should establish an approach for validating the work included in the sprint.

This may include:

- Functional testing activities
- Regression testing activities
- Integration testing activities
- Automation updates
- Non-functional testing activities
- Exploratory testing efforts
- User acceptance support

Testing activities should align with sprint objectives, risk levels, and release expectations.

---

### Identify Risks and Dependencies

Quality professionals should actively identify:

- Business risks
- Technical risks
- Quality risks
- Environmental risks
- Data dependencies
- Cross-team dependencies

Risk identification should occur before implementation begins whenever possible.

---

### Align Validation Efforts with Development Plans

Quality and development strategies should support one another.

Quality professionals should understand:

- Planned implementation approaches
- Development sequencing
- Technical design decisions
- Areas of complexity
- Areas of uncertainty

This alignment improves collaboration and allows testing activities to be performed more effectively.

---

### Coordinate Cross-Team Testing Activities

When sprint work impacts additional systems, business units, or delivery teams, quality professionals should help coordinate:

- Integration testing activities
- End-to-end testing efforts
- Shared environment usage
- Test data needs
- Release readiness validation

Cross-team coordination should occur early enough to avoid delivery delays.

---

### Validate Testing Readiness

Quality professionals should help ensure:

- Acceptance criteria are clear
- Testing approaches are identified
- Environments are available
- Test data needs are understood
- Dependencies are visible
- Validation activities can be executed within sprint timelines

Readiness reduces uncertainty and increases delivery confidence.

---

## Expected Outputs

Sprint Planning should provide enough information for quality professionals to establish:

- Sprint-level testing plans
- Validation strategies
- Risk assessments
- Dependency tracking
- Automation priorities
- End-to-end testing coordination needs
- Release validation activities
- Environment and test data requirements

These plans may evolve throughout the sprint as additional information becomes available.

---

## Guiding Questions for Quality Professionals

During sprint planning, quality professionals should continuously seek answers to the following questions.

### Outcome

- What is the team trying to accomplish this sprint?
- What defines success?

### Consumer

- Who benefits from this work?
- What expectations must be met?

### Constraints

- What limitations, dependencies, or risks exist?

### Structure

- How will the solution be implemented?
- What systems and teams are involved?

### Failure Modes

- What could prevent successful delivery?
- What introduces the greatest risk?

### Verification

- How will confidence be established?
- What evidence will demonstrate that expectations have been met?

These questions help ensure quality activities remain focused on delivering confidence and reducing uncertainty.

---

## Success Criteria

Effective quality engagement during sprint planning results in:

- Clear sprint objectives and expectations
- Better visibility into risks and dependencies
- Appropriate testing and validation planning
- Improved coordination across teams
- Increased readiness for implementation and testing
- Reduced sprint surprises and rework
- Greater confidence in sprint commitments
- Improved release readiness

The goal of quality engagement during sprint planning is to ensure the team enters the sprint with a shared understanding of expectations, risks, validation plans, and delivery goals.
