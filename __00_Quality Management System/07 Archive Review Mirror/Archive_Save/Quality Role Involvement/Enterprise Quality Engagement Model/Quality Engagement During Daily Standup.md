# Quality Engagement During Daily Standups

## Purpose

Daily Standups provide Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals with an opportunity to remain aligned with sprint progress, identify emerging risks, coordinate quality activities, and maintain visibility into ongoing work.

Quality professionals should be active members of the delivery team and contribute to a shared understanding of progress, planned work, blockers, dependencies, and quality concerns.

The purpose of daily participation is not status reporting.

The purpose is to improve communication, surface risks early, remove obstacles, and maintain confidence in delivery progress throughout the sprint.

---

## Quality Objectives During Daily Standups

During daily standups, quality professionals should seek to understand:

- Current progress toward sprint goals
- Development activities currently in progress
- Emerging risks and concerns
- Blockers impacting delivery or validation activities
- Dependency changes
- Testing readiness
- Deployment and release considerations
- Areas requiring additional collaboration

Quality professionals should help ensure that quality-related concerns remain visible and addressed throughout the sprint.

---

## Recommended Quality Participation Opportunities

Quality professionals should actively participate in daily standups and contribute when quality-related information may help the team make informed decisions.

Examples include:

### Sharing Progress

Provide visibility into:

- Testing activities completed
- Validation efforts in progress
- Automation development activities
- Defect investigation efforts
- Environment readiness
- Test data preparation activities

The team should understand how quality activities are progressing alongside development work.

---

### Identifying Risks and Concerns

Raise any concerns related to:

- Testing readiness
- Environment issues
- Test data availability
- Defects or production issues
- Release risks
- Cross-team dependencies
- Delivery confidence

Early communication allows teams to address challenges before they become delivery obstacles.

---

### Escalating Blockers

Quality professionals should promptly communicate blockers impacting:

- Validation activities
- Automation development
- Environment access
- Dependency coordination
- Test execution
- Release readiness

Removing blockers quickly helps maintain sprint momentum.

---

### Coordinating Team Activities

Daily standups provide an opportunity to align activities across:

- Product Owners
- Software Engineers
- Architects
- Quality Professionals
- External teams and partners

Quality professionals should help ensure testing and validation activities remain synchronized with development progress.

---

## Quality Responsibilities During Daily Standups

### Maintain Visibility Into Quality Health

Quality professionals should help the team understand:

- Testing progress
- Current quality risks
- Open defects
- Validation status
- Readiness concerns

The goal is to provide transparency rather than act as a delivery gate.

---

### Promote Continuous Collaboration

Quality activities should occur throughout the sprint rather than at the end.

Quality professionals should encourage:

- Early feedback
- Ongoing validation
- Shared ownership of quality
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Rapid issue resolution

---

### Advocate for Quality Practices

Quality professionals should serve as advocates for activities that support successful delivery, including:

- Risk-based testing
- Automation opportunities
- Early validation
- Quality-focused discussions
- Continuous improvement

Quality advocacy should help teams improve outcomes while maintaining delivery momentum.

---

### Support Delivery Confidence

Daily conversations should help establish confidence that:

- Sprint objectives remain achievable
- Quality activities are progressing appropriately
- Risks are understood
- Validation efforts remain aligned with delivery goals

Confidence is created through visibility, communication, and collaboration.

---

## Guiding Questions for Quality Professionals

During daily standups, quality professionals should continuously consider the following questions:

### Outcome

- Is current work progressing toward sprint goals?

### Consumer

- Are any risks emerging that could impact users or stakeholders?

### Constraints

- What blockers or dependencies are affecting progress?

### Structure

- Are implementation activities proceeding as expected?

### Failure Modes

- What new risks or concerns have emerged since the previous standup?

### Verification

- Are validation activities keeping pace with development activities?

These questions help maintain visibility into quality throughout the sprint and support the Clayton Tech Quality Container framework.

---

## Success Criteria

Effective quality engagement during daily standups results in:

- Improved communication across the delivery team
- Earlier identification of risks and blockers
- Better alignment between development and quality activities
- Increased visibility into delivery progress
- Faster issue resolution
- Greater confidence in sprint execution
- Reduced surprises later in the sprint

The goal of quality engagement during daily standups is to maintain transparency, promote collaboration, and help teams continuously deliver progress with confidence
