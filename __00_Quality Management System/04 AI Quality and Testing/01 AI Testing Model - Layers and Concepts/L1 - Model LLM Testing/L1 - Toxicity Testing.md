# L1 — Toxicity Testing

## What

Toxicity Testing validates whether the model produces offensive, hostile, abusive, demeaning, harassing, or otherwise inappropriate language.

Toxicity is a specific safety concern focused on harmful language or tone.

---

## Why

Toxic outputs can harm users, damage trust, create workplace risk, and violate responsible AI expectations.

Toxicity Testing helps bound the risk of:
- Offensive responses
- Harassing language
- Demeaning content
- Hostile tone
- Inappropriate workplace communication
- User harm caused by generated language

---

## Where

Toxicity Testing applies to model-generated text.

It is used when evaluating:
- Chat responses
- Drafted messages
- Summaries
- Generated feedback
- User-facing responses
- Internal assistant outputs
- Any model output that may be read by a person

This test type belongs at L1 because it evaluates the language generated by the model itself.

---

## When

Toxicity Testing may occur:
- During model selection
- During safety evaluation
- Before releasing user-facing capabilities
- Before enabling free-form generation
- After prompt or model changes
- During monitoring of production outputs

---

## How

Toxicity may be validated through:
- Scenario-based testing
- Adversarial prompts
- Human review
- Automated toxicity classifiers where available
- Testing edge cases and misuse scenarios
- Reviewing tone and wording in generated outputs
- Evaluating whether the model de-escalates or redirects appropriately

---

## Failure Modes

Common failure modes include:
- Offensive wording
- Harassing content
- Demeaning or insulting language
- Escalating hostile user input
- Producing inappropriate jokes or commentary
- Reinforcing harmful stereotypes
- Failing to maintain professional tone
- Generating content unsuitable for workplace use

---

## Verification / Signals

Verification signals may include:
- Toxicity score
- Toxic output rate
- Human review results
- Number of critical toxicity failures
- Correct de-escalation rate
- Professional tone adherence rate
- Reduction in toxic outputs after guardrail improvements

---

## Key Note

Toxicity Testing is related to Safety Testing but should remain separate when language harm needs explicit evaluation.

A response may be non-toxic but still unsafe, and a response may be toxic even if it does not provide operationally dangerous instructions.
