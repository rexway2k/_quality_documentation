# Testing Strategy Overview

## Purpose

Testing is one of the primary mechanisms used to establish confidence that a solution meets expectations and is capable of delivering value to its consumers.

Testing does not create quality.

Quality is built through clear expectations, thoughtful design, collaborative delivery practices, risk management, and continuous improvement. Testing provides information that helps teams understand whether those expectations have been met.

The purpose of testing is to reduce uncertainty and provide objective information that supports delivery decisions.

---

## Testing Philosophy

- Quality is the fulfillment of expectations.
- Testing provides information about quality.
- Quality is everyone's responsibility.
- Testing should be aligned to risk.
- Earlier feedback is generally less expensive than later feedback.
- No single testing activity can provide complete confidence.
- Different testing approaches solve different problems.
- Automation supports quality but does not replace critical thinking.

The goal of testing is not to find every defect.

The goal of testing is to provide sufficient confidence that the solution can successfully fulfill its intended purpose.

---

## Testing as Risk Management

Testing should always be viewed through the lens of risk.

Not all defects have equal impact.

Not all systems require the same level of testing.

Not all testing provides the same value.

Testing strategies should consider:

- Business impact
- User impact
- Technical complexity
- Integration dependencies
- Security concerns
- Compliance requirements
- Operational risk
- Deployment risk

The greater the risk, the greater the validation effort may need to be.

---

## Testing Is Multi-Layered

Quality cannot be established through a single testing activity.

Testing occurs at multiple levels throughout the solution lifecycle.

Each level provides different types of feedback, different speed of feedback, and different confidence signals.

Clayton Tech utilizes a multi-level testing model (L0-L5) which supports prevention, validation, integration confidence, business process verification, and production confidence.

---

## Testing Is Continuous

Testing activities should occur throughout solution delivery.

Testing begins before code is written and continues after deployment.

Examples include:

- Requirements reviews
- Design reviews
- Risk assessments
- Unit testing
- Functional testing
- Integration testing
- End-to-End testing
- Release validation
- Production monitoring

The objective is continuous quality feedback throughout delivery.

---

## Testing Types and Testing Levels

One of the most common areas of confusion in software testing is the difference between testing levels and testing types.

### Testing Levels answer:

> Where is testing being performed?

Examples:

- Unit
- System
- Integration
- End-to-End
- Production Validation

### Testing Types answer:

> What characteristic are we evaluating?

Examples:

- Functional
- Performance
- Security
- Accessibility
- Operational

Both concepts are important and should be considered when designing testing strategies.

---

## Success Criteria

A successful testing strategy should:

- Align to business and consumer expectations
- Focus on risk reduction
- Provide rapid feedback
- Enable informed delivery decisions
- Improve delivery confidence
- Support continuous improvement

The purpose of testing is not simply execution.

The purpose of testing is to provide meaningful confidence that expectations have been met.
