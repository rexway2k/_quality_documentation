# Exploratory Testing

## Purpose

Exploratory Testing is an approach that combines learning, investigation, and testing simultaneously to discover risks, unexpected behaviors, and opportunities for improvement.

Unlike scripted testing, Exploratory Testing allows the tester to adapt based on observations, discoveries, and emerging information.

The objective is to identify risks and behaviors that may not have been anticipated during planning, design, or test case creation.

Exploratory Testing helps uncover the unknown.

---

## Key Question

> What could happen that we did not anticipate?

---

## What Exploratory Testing Validates

Exploratory Testing may evaluate:

- User behavior
- Workflow variations
- Unexpected inputs
- Edge cases
- Error handling
- System resilience
- Usability concerns
- Operational risks
- Business assumptions

Exploration helps teams learn about the solution while testing it.

---

## Why Exploratory Testing Matters

No test plan can predict every possible scenario.

No automated suite can anticipate every unexpected behavior.

No specification captures every real-world situation.

Exploratory Testing provides a structured way for quality professionals to investigate beyond known requirements.

This complements, rather than replaces, scripted and automated testing.

---

## Common Examples

Examples include:

- Following unusual navigation paths.
- Combining workflows in unexpected ways.
- Testing with uncommon data values.
- Simulating user mistakes and recovery actions.
- Investigating ambiguous or poorly defined requirements.
- Exploring newly released functionality in production-like environments.

---

## Common Defects Identified

Exploratory Testing often identifies:

- Edge case failures
- Usability concerns
- Workflow inconsistencies
- Unexpected system behaviors
- Error handling issues
- Requirement gaps
- Assumption failures
- Consumer experience concerns

These issues are frequently difficult to discover using scripted testing alone.

---

## L0-L5 Alignment

Exploratory Testing can occur throughout solution delivery.

| Testing Level | Typical Usage |
|--------------|--------------|
| L0 | Review and analysis activities |
| L1 | Limited applicability |
| L2 | Feature exploration |
| L3 | Integration exploration |
| L4 | Business process exploration |
| L5 | Production validation and investigation |

Most exploratory testing occurs at L2, L3, and L4.

---

## Exploratory Testing Principles

### Curiosity

Effective exploratory testing begins with a willingness to investigate and learn.

---

### Critical Thinking

Exploration requires continuous evaluation of:

- Risk
- Behavior
- Assumptions
- Outcomes

---

### Adaptability

The tester adapts based on discoveries made during testing.

Each observation may influence the next action.

---

### Learning

The objective is not simply execution.

The objective is learning about the solution and identifying risks.

---

### Documentation

While exploration may be flexible, observations and findings should still be documented appropriately.

---

## Relationship to Automation

Exploratory Testing and automation serve different purposes.

Automation helps:

- Execute known checks
- Improve consistency
- Increase repeatability
- Accelerate feedback

Exploratory Testing helps:

- Discover unknown risks
- Investigate unexpected behavior
- Challenge assumptions
- Improve understanding

Automation preserves clarity.

Exploration expands understanding.

Both are valuable components of an effective testing strategy.

---

## Risks Reduced

Exploratory Testing helps reduce:

- Unknown risks
- Usability concerns
- Requirement misunderstandings
- Consumer experience issues
- Unexpected production behaviors

---

## Success Criteria

Exploratory Testing is successful when:

- New information is learned
- Risks are identified
- Assumptions are challenged
- Opportunities for improvement are discovered
- Confidence in the solution increases

The purpose of Exploratory Testing is not simply to execute tests.

The purpose is to learn, investigate, and uncover information that helps improve quality and reduce uncertainty.
