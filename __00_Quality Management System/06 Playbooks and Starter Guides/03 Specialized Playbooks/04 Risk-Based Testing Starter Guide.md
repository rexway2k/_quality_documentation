# Risk-Based Testing Starter Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps teams focus their validation effort on the areas that matter most.

## Core Idea

Testing should be guided by risk, not by habit or volume alone.

## Risk Dimensions to Consider

- business impact
- user impact
- technical complexity
- integration dependencies
- security and compliance concerns
- operational sensitivity

## Practical Approach

1. Identify the main outcome that matters
2. Identify what could go wrong
3. Prioritize the areas that would create the greatest impact if they failed
4. Choose the right level and method of validation

## Simple Rule

Higher risk means earlier and broader validation.

Lower risk means lighter validation is often appropriate.

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The highest-impact risks are known
- [ ] The validation effort matches the risk
- [ ] The team is not over-testing low-risk work
- [ ] The team is not under-testing high-risk work
