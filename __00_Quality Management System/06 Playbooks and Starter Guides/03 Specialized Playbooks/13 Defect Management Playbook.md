# Defect Management Playbook

## Purpose

This guide helps teams manage defects in a way that supports learning, decision-making, and risk reduction.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this throughout the defect lifecycle, from capture and triage through resolution, verification, and closure.

## Core Idea

Defects are information. They should help teams understand quality risk, improve delivery, and reduce recurrence.

## Practical Lifecycle Guidance

### Capture
Record the issue with enough context to understand the problem.

### Triage
Assess impact, urgency, and whether the defect blocks delivery or release.

### Resolve
Implement and verify the fix with attention to the intended outcome.

### Learn
Review the defect to understand what it tells the team about risk, process, or validation gaps.

## Minimum Defect Content

Every defect should include:
- clear summary
- expected behavior
- actual behavior
- reproduction information
- environment details where relevant
- severity and impact
- follow-up or root cause notes where known

## Common Mistakes

- poor defect detail
- treating defect tracking as only a workflow task
- failing to review defects for broader learning
- missing the link between defects and future prevention

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The defect is clear and actionable
- [ ] Impact and urgency are understood
- [ ] The team knows the next step and owner
- [ ] The lesson from the defect is captured
