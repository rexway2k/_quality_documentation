# Root Cause Analysis Starter Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps teams perform lightweight, practical root cause analysis when a defect, incident, or quality issue needs deeper understanding.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this when a defect is recurring, high impact, or has exposed a gap in how the team works.

## Core Idea

The goal is not blame. The goal is to understand what contributed to the issue and what should change to reduce recurrence.

## Simple Investigation Flow

1. Describe what happened
2. Identify the impact and severity
3. Review the contributing factors
4. Determine whether the cause was technical, process, architectural, environmental, or data-related
5. Define corrective and preventive actions

## Useful Questions

- What was expected?
- What happened instead?
- What assumptions or conditions were in place?
- What part of the process or system allowed this to happen?
- What should change so it is less likely to recur?

## Common Mistakes

- stopping at the symptom
- treating the issue as only a coding problem
- failing to connect the analysis to prevention
- skipping the follow-up action

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The issue is clearly described
- [ ] The impact is understood
- [ ] The likely contributing factors are identified
- [ ] The team has agreed actions to reduce recurrence
