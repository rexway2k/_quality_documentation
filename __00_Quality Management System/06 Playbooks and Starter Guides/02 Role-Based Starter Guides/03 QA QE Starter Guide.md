# QA/QE Starter Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps QA and Quality Engineering professionals apply the quality model in a practical, collaborative way.

## What You Need to Know

QA/QE is not only about execution. It is also about helping teams understand risk, define validation, and improve confidence throughout delivery.

## What You Should Do

- help clarify the quality intent and the key risks
- support meaningful validation planning
- identify where automated, manual, or exploratory validation fits best
- contribute to release readiness conversations
- help capture learning from defects and incidents

## Questions to Ask

- What matters most here?
- What could fail in a meaningful way?
- Where is the best place to build confidence?
- What evidence is enough for this change?
- What should we monitor after release?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- treating testing as a checklist exercise
- focusing only on execution without understanding intent
- over-testing low-risk work or under-testing high-risk work
- waiting until late in the cycle to raise quality concerns

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The intent is clear
- [ ] The key risks are understood
- [ ] Validation is matched to the risk
- [ ] The team has enough evidence to decide
- [ ] Release and support concerns are addressed
