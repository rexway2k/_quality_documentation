# Leadership and Delivery Manager Starter Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps leaders and delivery managers use quality concepts in a practical way without turning them into bureaucracy.

## What You Need to Know

Leadership contributes to quality by creating the conditions for good decisions, clear priorities, and realistic delivery expectations.

## What You Should Do

- support early risk discussion and clarity of goals
- help teams focus on the most important outcomes
- encourage validation that matches the level of risk
- make space for release readiness and incident learning
- avoid forcing unnecessary process overhead

## Questions to Ask

- What outcome matters most for this work?
- What is the main risk if this fails?
- What evidence do we need before release?
- Are we asking the team to do the right amount of validation?
- What must we learn from defects or incidents?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- treating every change as equally risky
- expecting quality to be solved only through testing activity
- adding process without clear value
- delaying decisions until late in delivery

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The priority is clear
- [ ] The main risk is visible
- [ ] The team has enough time for meaningful validation
- [ ] Release readiness is discussed before go-live
- [ ] Learning from issues is captured
