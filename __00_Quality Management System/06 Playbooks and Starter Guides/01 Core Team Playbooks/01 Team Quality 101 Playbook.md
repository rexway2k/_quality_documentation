# Team Quality 101 Playbook

## Purpose

This guide is for new team members and anyone who needs a simple, practical understanding of quality in delivery.

It explains the minimum ideas that matter most and the habits that help teams build confidence without overcomplicating the work.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this when:
- a new team member joins the team
- a team wants a shared starting point for quality
- someone needs a simple explanation of how quality connects to delivery

## Core Ideas

### 1. Quality is shared
Quality is not owned by one role. Product, engineering, QA, operations, and leadership all contribute.

### 2. Testing reduces uncertainty
Testing gives information about whether expectations are being met. It does not create quality by itself.

### 3. Risk should guide effort
The more important or risky the change, the more validation is usually needed.

### 4. Earlier feedback is cheaper
It is better to clarify expectations early than to discover issues late.

## Minimum Team Habits

Every team should do these consistently:
- clarify what success looks like
- define acceptance criteria before building
- identify the biggest risks early
- validate the important behaviors before release
- capture learning from defects and incidents

## What Good Quality Looks Like in Practice

A team is building quality when it can clearly answer:
- what outcome matters
- what could go wrong
- how we will know it worked
- what evidence we need before release

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The outcome is clear
- [ ] The main risks are understood
- [ ] The work is testable
- [ ] The team knows what evidence matters
- [ ] The team has a simple plan for validation
