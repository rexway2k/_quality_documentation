# Defect Triage and Root Cause Playbook

## Purpose

This guide helps teams respond to defects in a practical and useful way.

It supports quick triage, clear communication, and better learning from defects rather than simply logging them.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this when defects are reported, triaged, or investigated during development, release, or support.

## Triage Questions

When a defect appears, the team should quickly answer:
- what is the impact
- how severe is it
- what is the urgency
- what is the likely root cause area
- what action is needed next

## Practical Triage Approach

1. Describe the issue clearly
2. Identify the impacted behavior or user outcome
3. Assess impact and urgency
4. Decide whether the issue blocks release, delivery, or support
5. Identify the next action and owner

## Root Cause Mindset

The goal is not just to fix the symptom. The goal is to understand what allowed the issue to happen and what should change to reduce recurrence.

Useful root cause questions include:
- what was misunderstood
- what assumption failed
- what validation gap allowed this to pass
- what process or design weakness contributed

## Common Mistakes

- logging defects without enough context
- treating all defects as equal in importance
- fixing the symptom without understanding the cause
- failing to capture learning for prevention

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Impact is understood
- [ ] Severity and urgency are considered
- [ ] The defect is clearly described
- [ ] Owner and next step are clear
- [ ] Root cause or contributing factors are explored
