# Story and Backlog Quality Playbook

## Purpose

This guide helps teams prepare work so it can be built, tested, and delivered with less rework.

It focuses on making stories understandable, testable, and aligned to risk.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this during backlog refinement, story preparation, and planning conversations.

## Before a Story Is Ready

A story is easier to deliver when the team can answer these questions:
- what outcome matters
- what is in scope and what is out of scope
- what could go wrong
- how will we know it worked
- what evidence is needed

## Make the Work Testable

Good stories usually include:
- a clear user or business outcome
- clear acceptance criteria
- key edge cases or failure scenarios
- known dependencies and assumptions
- an idea of the validation approach

## Questions to Ask in Refinement

- Is the requirement clear enough to build?
- What would make this fail?
- What is the highest-risk part of this work?
- Is there anything that needs early validation?
- Do we know how to test it?

## Simple Readiness Signals

A story is closer to ready when:
- the scope is clear
- the acceptance criteria are understandable
- the key risks are identified
- the team knows how to validate it
- the work is not overly ambiguous

## Common Mistakes

- leaving ambiguity in the acceptance criteria
- treating everything as equally important
- skipping risk discussion
- assuming the work is testable without defining the validation path

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Outcome is clear
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are understandable
- [ ] Main risks are called out
- [ ] Validation approach is discussed
- [ ] Dependencies and assumptions are visible
