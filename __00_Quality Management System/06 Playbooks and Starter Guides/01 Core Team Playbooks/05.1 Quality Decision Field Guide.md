# Quality Decision Field Guide

## Purpose

This guide translates the core quality decision mindset into a practical decision tool for planning, testing, review, and release conversations.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this when the team needs to make quality decisions clearly and consistently, especially around testing intent, risk, validation approach, and confidence.

## Start Here: Define the Contract

Before testing or planning begins, clarify:
- what outcome must be true
- who depends on it
- what constraints must hold
- how it could fail
- how the team will prove it works

If these are unclear, return to the contract before choosing tests or validation methods.

## The Five Decision Dimensions

Every quality decision should address five dimensions:

### Why
What decision are we trying to make? What risk are we trying to reduce?

### What
What type of risk matters most?

Examples include:
- functional correctness
- performance
- security
- reliability
- usability
- compliance

### Where
Where should confidence be built?

Examples include:
- unit validation
- service validation
- integration validation
- end-to-end validation
- business or user validation

### How
How will the team validate efficiently?

Examples include:
- manual validation
- automated validation
- semi-automated validation
- monitoring
- synthetic checks

### When
When must this risk be reduced?

Examples include:
- discovery and design
- refinement
- build
- release and production

## Governing Rule

A test plan is not just a list of tests. It should answer:
- why are we testing
- what risk matters
- where we validate
- how we validate
- when it happens

If you cannot explain all five, the plan is incomplete.

## Risk Drives Everything

These dimensions work together to bound risk:
- why defines the decision
- what defines the risk type
- where defines the validation level
- how defines the execution method
- when defines the timing

## What “Enough” Looks Like

Validation is sufficient when:
- critical risks are identified
- potential failure modes are understood
- evidence shows those risks are controlled

It is not sufficient merely to test everything or to increase volume without clarity.

## Quick Check

Before executing, the team should be able to explain the decision in one line:

Intent | Attribute | Level | Execution Mode | Timing
