# Quality Adoption Overview

## Purpose

This guide helps teams introduce the playbooks and starter guides in a lightweight, practical way.

It is meant to support adoption without creating a heavy rollout process.

## When to Use This Guide

Use this when introducing the content to a team, new squad, or delivery group.

## Suggested Adoption Approach

### 1. Start with the most useful guides
Begin with:
- Team Quality 101 Playbook
- What to Test, Where, When, and How Playbook
- Story and Backlog Quality Playbook
- Release Readiness Playbook

### 2. Use them in real moments
The best adoption happens when the guides are used in planning, refinement, release review, and incident response rather than read in isolation.

### 3. Keep it lightweight
Teams do not need a formal training program to benefit from these guides. A short walkthrough is often enough.

### 4. Tie the guides to real work
Connect each guide to a specific workflow such as backlog refinement, testing planning, release review, or defect triage.

## Suggested Rollout Pattern

- introduce the basics in a short team session
- point people to the guides that match their current work
- use the checklists during real meetings
- review what helped and what felt too heavy

## Common Adoption Mistakes

- trying to roll out everything at once
- making the guides feel like formal policy
- expecting teams to read all content before using any of it
- failing to connect the guides to actual team ceremonies

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] The team knows where to start
- [ ] The guides are tied to real workflows
- [ ] The content feels lightweight and useful
- [ ] Teams can use the materials immediately
