# Enterprise Quality Engagement Model

## Purpose

This framework defines how Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Engineering (QE) professionals engage throughout the solution delivery lifecycle at Clayton Tech.

The purpose of this guidance is to establish common expectations, promote consistent quality practices, and help delivery teams understand the opportunities for quality involvement throughout planning, development, testing, deployment, and operational support activities.

While each team may adopt delivery practices that best fit its specific needs, this framework establishes a common foundation that can be tailored through team-level working agreements and delivery processes.

The goal is not to create additional process or overhead.

The goal is to ensure quality activities occur early, consistently, and collaboratively so that teams can deliver valuable solutions with confidence.

---

## Quality Engagement Philosophy

Quality is not a phase.

Quality is not a gate.

Quality is not the responsibility of a single role or team.

Quality is a shared commitment to meeting expectations and delivering outcomes that create value for our consumers.

Quality professionals serve as advocates, facilitators, coaches, engineers, and partners throughout solution delivery. Their responsibility is to help teams establish clear expectations, identify and manage risk, improve delivery practices, and build confidence in the solutions being delivered.

The earlier quality is involved, the more effectively risk can be identified, assumptions can be challenged, and defects can be prevented.

---

## Expectations of Quality Professionals

Quality Assurance and Quality Engineering professionals are expected to be active participants throughout the software development lifecycle.

Quality team members should:

- Actively engage in team discussions and delivery activities
- Advocate for quality practices and continuous improvement
- Seek clarity when requirements, expectations, or acceptance criteria are unclear
- Understand the business objectives and consumer needs behind delivered solutions
- Identify risks, assumptions, constraints, and dependencies early
- Promote collaboration across teams, systems, and business processes
- Support informed decision-making through objective quality insights
- Drive proactive quality activities rather than waiting for testing phases
- Continuously improve quality processes, practices, and outcomes

Quality professionals should proactively seek involvement when they do not have the information necessary to successfully evaluate risk, support delivery, or establish confidence in a solution.

---

## Quality Involvement Across the Delivery Lifecycle

Quality engagement should occur throughout the delivery lifecycle rather than being concentrated near the end of development.

The following sections outline recommended quality involvement during key delivery activities:

### New Project Planning

Participate in the early planning of projects, initiatives, and large enhancements to ensure quality expectations, risks, dependencies, and validation strategies are considered from the beginning.

### Backlog Refinement and Estimation

Collaborate with Product Owners and delivery teams to review requirements, identify risks and assumptions, clarify acceptance criteria, and evaluate testability before development begins.

### Sprint Planning

Partner with the delivery team to understand upcoming work, identify required quality activities, evaluate dependencies, and ensure testing and validation efforts are appropriately planned.

### Daily Standups

Remain informed of development progress, identify emerging risks or blockers, coordinate testing efforts, and ensure visibility into quality-related concerns.

### In-Sprint Development and Testing Activities

Collaborate continuously with Product Owners, Engineers, Architects, and stakeholders to support validation, automation, risk assessment, defect prevention, and quality improvements throughout implementation.

### Release Planning and Deployment

Provide visibility into quality outcomes, risks, coverage, testing results, and deployment readiness to support informed release decisions.

### Production Support and Continuous Improvement

Monitor quality signals, investigate issues, conduct root cause analysis, identify improvement opportunities, and incorporate lessons learned into future delivery efforts.

---

## Expected Outcome

Successful quality engagement should result in:

- Clearer expectations and requirements
- Earlier identification of risks and dependencies
- Reduced defects and production incidents
- Improved collaboration across teams
- Increased confidence in delivery decisions
- Better customer and end-user outcomes
- Faster feedback loops
- Greater visibility into quality health and delivery performance
- Continuous improvement of products, processes, and practices

The objective of quality involvement is not to control delivery.

The objective is to improve confidence, reduce uncertainty, and help teams consistently deliver solutions that meet expectations.
